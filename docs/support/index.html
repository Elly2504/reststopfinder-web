Frequently Asked Questions

How does RestStop Finder find rest stops?

The app uses data from OpenStreetMap, a community-driven mapping project, to locate rest areas along US highways. Data is updated regularly by contributors worldwide.

Why does the app need my location?

Your location is used to show rest stops near you and calculate distances. Location data is processed entirely on your device and is never sent to our servers.

What is "Always" location permission for?

If you enable background notifications, the app uses iOS region monitoring to alert you when approaching a rest stop — even when the app is closed. This feature is optional and can be disabled in Settings. The app works fully with "When In Use" location permission.

Can I use the app without location access?

The app requires location access to find rest stops near you. If location permission is denied, the app will show a prompt explaining how to enable it in iOS Settings.

How do I clear cached data?

Go to the Settings tab in the app and tap "Clear Cache." This removes temporarily stored rest stop data. Your favorites are not affected.

Is my data shared with anyone?

No. RestStop Finder does not collect personal information, has no accounts, no ads, no analytics, and no tracking. All data stays on your device. See our Privacy Policy for details.
